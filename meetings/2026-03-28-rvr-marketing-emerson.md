## Discussion Points

### 1. Content Strategy — What Actually Converts?

The NSFW-adjacent market responds to:
- **Sample content** — short audio/TTS previews that showcase what the product does
- **Story teasers** — intrigue without full explicit content
- **Community engagement** — replying to comments, DMs, building relationships
- **Free Vault** — landing page that converts free users to paid

**Questions for Emerson:**
- Which channel do we double down on first?
- What content format drives the best conversion for adult/NSFW-adjacent audiences?
- Should we be doing content seeding on platforms we haven't tried?

---

### 2. Paid Ads — What Are We Even Buying?

With $100 split across 3 platforms, we're not buying volume — we're buying **signal**.

**What we want from the $100:**
- Learn which platform's audience actually converts
- Find the 18-35 male demographic that's interested in AI character chat
- Get initial subscriber base to hit the $100/month Patreon revenue mark

**Questions for Emerson:**
- Is $100/month even meaningful for paid ads? Or is it better spent on one platform for a focused test?
- Which platform has the best ad tooling for adult-adjacent content? (Reddit? X? Others?)
- Should we be running "dark" ads (no branded content, just landing page links)?

---

### 3. Conversion Funnel — Where Do We Lose People?

Current funnel:
```
Platform (Reddit/DeviantArt/Literotica)
  ↓ click
rvr.chat or redvelvetrenders.com
  ↓ sign up
Free account (30 credits/day)
  ↓ ... how do we convert?
Paid subscriber ($3-9/month)
```

**Questions for Emerson:**
- What's the typical conversion rate from free → paid in our market?
- How do we加速 the free → paid decision? (What triggers someone to pay?)
- Is Patreon the right platform, or should we consider direct credit purchases?

---

### 4. The "Free Vault" Strategy

We have a "Free Vault" landing page that converts free users to paid. The idea:
- Give users a taste (free credits)
- Let the product speak for itself
- Convert when they run out of free credits

**Questions for Emerson:**
- Is this the right conversion mechanism, or should we A/B test different CTAs?
- What's the optimal free credit amount to give without devaluing the paid product?

---

### 5. Content Calendar — What Do We Post?

3 posts/week per channel × 3 channels = 9 pieces of content/week

**Content pillars:**
1. **TTS audio samples** — short clips showcasing voice quality
2. **Story teasers** — intrigue from RVR's existing content (Kaliyuga Charitham, Mind Control)
3. **Community highlights** — (with permission) user success stories, interesting chats
4. **Behind the scenes** — how the tech works, AI character creation process

**Questions for Emerson:**
- What's the optimal posting frequency for each platform?
- Should we batch-create content (1 day/week) or spread it out?
